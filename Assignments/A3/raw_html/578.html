Go curling in Denver during the 2018 Winter Olympics

brandsauthority 1 week ago World News Leave a comment 38 Views

China’s Ba Dexin, right prepares to throw the stone beside teammate Wang Rui during their mixed doubles curling match against Olympic Athletes from Russia at the 2018 Winter Olympics in Gangneung, South Korea, Friday, Feb. 9, 2018. (Aaron Favila, The Associated Press)

So, you’ve been watching the Olympics on TV or online and now you’ve got “curling fever” and want to try to “throw a rock at a house,” eh?

Stop by the Denver Curling Club and give it your best slide!

The Denver Curling Club is hosting public open houses Feb. 10, 11, 13, 17 and 18 that include on-ice instruction, mini games, live game demonstrations and broadcasts of Olympic competitions from Pyeongchang.

Club members will be on hand before, during and after instruction to answer questions about the game, strategy, equipment and how you can get involved in the sport.

The Denver Curling Club holds regular recreational and competitive leagues for children, teens and adults, fall through late spring.

Olympics Open House instruction (for ages 13 and older) and a mini game costs $20 and includes a soft drink.

30-minute drop-in instructional sessions are scheduled:

10 a.m.-2 p.m. Saturday, Feb. 10;
2-4 p.m. Sunday, Feb. 11;
10 a.m.-4 p.m. and 7-9 p.m. Saturday, Feb. 17; and
2-4 p.m. Sunday, Feb. 18.

Kids only

There will be a special drop-in instructional session for kids ages 6-12 from 9 to 10 a.m. Saturday, Feb. 17.

60-minute pre-registered instructional sessions are scheduled:

5 p.m., 6 p.m. and 7 p.m. Tuesday, Feb. 13. (Cost is $30.)
Participants at all drop-in sessions receive an overview of curling rules and strategy as well as instruction and practice of basic delivery and sweeping skills.

Entry to the club’s “warm room,” with large windows overlooking the ice and closed-circuit TV coverage of each local game, is free.

What to bring:

Bring clean, rubber-soled shoes and warm, comfortable clothing (think layers). Children attending the Feb. 17 event must bring and wear a sports helmet. All curling equipment will be provided.

Learn to Curl Classes

Two-hour learn to curl classes with more in-depth and personalized instruction will be held 7-9 p.m. April 9, 10, 11 and 12. Cost is $40. Pre-registration is required.

New and Experienced Curler Mini Leagues

Five- and six-week leagues for new and experienced curlers will start in February and April. Cost is $130 and includes weekly instruction with experienced curlers and weekly games. Pre-registration is required.

New Curlers: 1:30-3:30 p.m. Tuesdays, beginning Feb. 27

New and Experienced Curlers: 6:15-8:15 p.m. Mondays, beginning April 16

New Curlers: 6:15-8:15 p.m. Wednesdays, beginning April 18

New and Experienced Curlers: 8:30-10:30 p.m. Wednesdays, beginning April 16

Information and Registration:

The Denver Curling Club is a nonprofit group based at the Denver Curling Center, 14100 W. 7th Ave. in Golden (off U.S. 6 at Indiana Street). See details at denvercurlingclub.com or call 303-321-1107.
