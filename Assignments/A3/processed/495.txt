SEOUL, Feb. 10 (Yonhap) -- North Korea's official media on Saturday carried reports on the opening of the PyeongChang Olympics in South Korea on Friday and the North's ceremonial head of state Kim Yong-nam's meeting with South Korean President Moon Jae-in during the opening events.
In a brief dispatch from Pyongyang, the Korean Central News Agency (KCNA) reported that Kim, president of the Presidium of the Supreme People's Assembly, who is heading the North's high-level delegation, attended the pre-opening ceremony reception held before the 23rd Winter Olympics and met with Moon.
"Kim exchanged warm greetings with Moon and had a photo session with him. He also exchanged greetings with heads of states and governments of different countries who came to attend the opening ceremony of the Olympics at the reception," the report said.
In a separate dispatch, the KCNA reported on the opening ceremony of the PyeongChang Olympics, as well as the attendance of the North's delegates, which include Kim Yo-jong, the sister of North Korean leader Kim Jong-un.
It also mentioned the joint march by South and North Korean athletes under the Korean Peninsula flag, saying that "spectators broke into cheers and applause" as sportspeople of both Koreas entered the stadium to the song "Arirang."
   The report also added that Kim Yo-jong exchanged "warm greetings" with President Moon during the opening ceremony.
